The five rules of the game

Rule 1 is how money is made. Rules 2–5 are how it's kept.
1 · The profit test — (how often right × avg win) − (how often wrong × avg loss) − fees
This number must be positive or the strategy loses money, full stop. Everything we test is judged by this.
2 · The luck check — more bets = more certainty
Being right 55% of the time over 20 trades could easily be luck. Over 500 trades, it's real. That's why we wait for ~40 checked predictions before judging.
3 · The smoothness score — profit ÷ how bumpy the ride is
Two strategies can make the same money; the one with smaller stomach-drops is better, because you'll actually stick with it.
4 · Bet sizing — never bet so big that a losing streak ends the game
Even a genuinely winning strategy ruins you if each bet is too large. This killed more traders than bad predictions ever did.
5 · The hole rule — lose 50% and you need +100% just to get back
Avoiding big losses matters more than chasing big wins. Holes are easier to avoid than to climb out of.

House rules
  1. Decide how an idea will be judged before testing it. Never adjust afterwards to make it look better.
  2. AI predictions can't be tested on the past — the AI already knows how the past turned out. Live test only.
  3. Fees and costs are part of every result. An idea that only works with zero fees doesn't work.
  4. Practice money first, small real money later, bigger only after it keeps working.
  5. Most savings belong in boring index funds. These experiments stay small until proven.
